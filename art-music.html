Art & Music of the UAE

Traditional Dances

Dance is a living expression of Emirati pride and heritage. Men often perform Al-Ayyala/Yowlah in rhythmic rows with bamboo canes to drums and chants. Women perform graceful dances such as Al-Nashat and Al-Ruwahi, with flowing abayas and expressive hair movements during celebrations.

The National Anthem — Ishy Bilady

Officially adopted in 1971. Music by Mohammed Abdel Wahab. Lyrics added in 1986 by Emirati poet Arif Al Sheikh Abdullah Al Hassan.

The UAE Flag

Designed in 1971 by Abdullah Mohammed Al Maainah. Raised on December 2, 1971 (UAE National Day).

  • Red — Courage & sacrifice
  • Green — Growth & prosperity
  • White — Peace & honesty
  • Black — Strength & triumph over injustice

Emirati Artists

Pioneers include Abdul Qader Al Rais (watercolors, calligraphy + abstraction) and Najat Makki (vivid color fields and symbolism). Contemporary art thrives in venues like Alserkal Avenue, Manarat Al Saadiyat, and national museums.

Emirati Singers & Songs

Hussain Al Jassmi (e.g., Boshret Kheir, Foq), Ahlam Al Shamsi, Balqees Fathi, and Mehad Hamad blend traditional melodies with modern arrangements.
