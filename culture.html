Culture of the UAE

UAE National Day

Celebrated each year to mark the union of the Emirates, National Day is a time of parades, flags, traditional performances, and family gatherings. It highlights unity, pride, and the country’s cultural heritage—from songs and poetry to traditional dress.

Falconry

Falconry is one of the UAE’s oldest traditions, symbolizing strength, patience, and deep respect for nature. Passed down through generations, it represents a unique bond between humans and birds of prey and remains a celebrated heritage sport recognized by UNESCO.

Henna Art

Henna holds a special place in Emirati culture, used to decorate hands and feet during weddings and festivals. The intricate designs are symbols of beauty, joy, and blessings, reflecting centuries-old artistic traditions that continue to thrive in modern celebrations.

Traditional Dhow Boats

The wooden dhow represents the UAE’s maritime heritage. Once used for pearl diving and trade across the Arabian Gulf, it reflects the Emirati spirit of exploration and craftsmanship. Today, restored dhows stand as proud symbols of the nation’s seafaring history.

Gahwa

Gahwa—light Arabic coffee often flavored with cardamom—is traditionally served in a dallah and poured into small cups (finjān). Offering coffee to guests is a warm gesture of hospitality and respect at the heart of Emirati social life.

Al-Ayyala

Al-Ayyala is a well-known group performance where rows of men move in unison to drums and chanted poetry, symbolizing community, courage, and celebration. You’ll often see it at national events, weddings, and cultural festivals across the Emirates.
